The big three

Hydration, fiber (favor soluble/psyllium; piling on insoluble fiber can backfire and bloat), and physical activity. Cheap, safe, low yield — but it's where everyone starts.

AGA/ACG 2023 — fiber: conditional, very-low certaintylifestyle = first step, limited data

⚠ First: make sure it's not the badness

Before you push anything pro-motility, exclude ileus, mechanical obstruction, and other ominous causes. All these laxatives are contraindicated in known/suspected obstruction.

Osmotic, well tolerated, the evidence darling. Start 17 g daily and step up in 17 g increments to effect.

Senna the push 8.6 mg sennosides / tab

You'll order it as Senokot — a stimulant and a natural product of the desert. Chronic use paints the colon: melanosis coli — benign, reversible anthraquinone pigment, not premalignant.

AGA/ACG 2023 — senna: recommended

Double it Stack a second osmotic and bring in a heavier stimulant. We are escalating.

Mg citrate osmotic · "tastes good"*

Saline osmotic, brisk. (*citation needed on "tastes good.") Magnesium oxide also earns a nod for chronic use.

AGA/ACG 2023 — Mg oxide: conditional

Lactulose ~15 g "shooters"

Works, but PEG beats it head-to-head for stool frequency, form, and need for rescue meds — and even in acute HE, where PEG resolved encephalopathy in 91% vs 52% for lactulose.

Cochrane 2010 · PEG > lactuloseHELP trial · JAMA IM 2014 · acute HE

Bisacodyl — break the damn ✦ magic bullet PO 5–10 mg · PR 5–15 mg

Diphenylmethane stimulant. The oral works overnight; the rectal "Magic Bullet" (Dulcolax; supp commonly 10 mg) is your fast pass.

Kamm · Clin Gastro Hepatol 2011 (RCT)
Two osmotics + the Magic Bullet
Bisacodyl: for when the colon stops returning your calls and you need to break the seal on negotiations.

Go rectal Forces are mobilized. We approach from below — and we screen for two special situations.
MIL CODE FAST PACE · GI CODE BREACH PROTOCOL · color: RED

Enemas rescue therapy

Tap water or glycerin / oil (lactulose is fine too). Restrict enemas to rescue, not routine.

AGA OIC 2019 — enemas = rescue only

⚠ Please — no soap suds

Soap-suds enemas can trigger chemical colitis and mucosal injury. Ever had soap rubbed in your mouth? Neither has your rectum, and it would like to keep it that way.

☠ Fleet (sodium phosphate) can kill the kidney

Oral/rectal sodium phosphate can cause acute phosphate nephropathy — genuinely dangerous in ESRD / CKD, the elderly, and the volume-depleted.

FDA safety warning · nephrocalcinosisavoid in renal impairment

Side Branch · DEFCON 2A
2A
Opioid-induced? → PAMORAs Peripheral mu-opioid antagonists block gut receptors and reverse constipation without touching analgesia.
First: an adequate trial of ≥2 laxative classes, scheduled (not PRN). Fiber doesn't help OIC.

Naloxegol PO daily

Often the cheapest and just as effective.

AGA OIC 2019 — STRONG rec

Naldemedine PO daily

Underused. Strong evidence (COMPOSE 1/2/3).

AGA OIC 2019 — STRONG, high-quality

Methylnaltrexone SC or PO

Works, but weaker evidence base.

AGA OIC 2019 — conditional

Mechanism in one line

Block peripheral mu-opioid receptors in the gut wall → restore motility and secretion, while the central analgesia stays intact. Don't cross the blood-brain barrier.

Crockett et al · Gastroenterology 2019
Reach for the PAMORA before the fifth round of senna. Naloxegol is cheap; your patient's dignity is not.
Side Branch · DEFCON 2B
2B
Chronic / refractory? → Secretagogues OTC stuff failed? Step up to prescription pro-secretory / prokinetic agents.
No head-to-heads against the traditional agents → that's why you step up only after the cheap stuff fails (cost & access).

Linaclotide GC-C agonist

Pulls chloride & water into the lumen.

AGA/ACG 2023

Lubiprostone ClC-2 activator

AGA/ACG 2023

Prucalopride 5-HT₄ agonist

A prokinetic — drives colonic transit.

AGA/ACG 2023

The practical point

These are effective but pricey and were studied vs placebo, not vs PEG/senna. So: exhaust the cheap, evidence-backed workhorses first, then escalate here for genuinely refractory CIC.

Expensive ≠ better-than-PEG. It means "we never ran that trial." Earn the prior-auth.

The nuclear options Diplomacy has failed. We are no longer negotiating. Evacuate.
MIL CODE COCKED PISTOL · GI CODE FULL EVAC · color: WHITE

"Go-not-so-lytely" PEG-electrolyte lavage

Full-volume PEG + electrolytes (GoLYTELY-style). Carries a non-zero aspiration risk — mind the airway, the obtunded, and any whiff of ileus/obstruction.

Disimpaction — manual (please, no) vs endoscopic

Manual disimpaction when you must; endoscopic decompression / disimpaction when the situation (or the dignity) demands escalation.

Neostigmine 2 mg IV · for ACPO

For acute colonic pseudo-obstruction (Ogilvie) — and it's dramatic: 10/11 responded, median 4 minutes. Use cardiac monitoring (bradycardia), have atropine ready, and exclude mechanical obstruction first.

Ponec et al · NEJM 1999 (RCT)monitor for bradycardia
